The Fighter

Born in Arecibo. Built for war.

Nestor Bravo Velazquez carries the proud boxing tradition of Puerto Rico every time he steps through the ropes. Born October 6, 1993 in Arecibo, Bravo turned professional in 2015 at age 21 and announced himself immediately — a first-round knockout in his debut.

What followed was a decade-long campaign through the lightweight and super lightweight divisions: 24 wins, 17 by knockout, and a fan-friendly style built on a devastating left hook and relentless pressure.

After suffering his first defeat in a razor-thin split decision in March 2025, Bravo answered every question about his heart on January 10, 2026 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn — flattening battle-tested veteran Pedro Campa (37-4-1) with a second-round left hook that instantly made highlight reels around the world.

Now ranked among the most dangerous men at 140 pounds, Bravo is chasing the belt that Arecibo has been waiting for. 🔜 Campeón Mundial.

The Story

The Journey

2004 – 2010

A Kid With Gloves

Baseball and boxing filled his childhood — but the ring won. Nestor José Bravo Velázquez became a Florida P.A.L. State Champion (2004–2009) and a Junior Olympic State and Regional Champion, stacking titles before he could drive.

2011 – 2014

Beating Future Olympians

In the 2011 José "Cheo" Aponte International final, Bravo defeated Robson Conceição — the man who would win Olympic gold in 2016. He added Pre-Olympic gold in Trinidad & Tobago (2012), medals in the Dominican Republic, and silver at the Wilfredo Gómez Cup, finishing his amateur career 62-8.

2015

Turning Pro in Puerto Rico

February 21, 2015 — Coliseo Pancho Deida, Hatillo. A first-round knockout in his pro debut. Within his first year he was fighting on Mega TV and DirecTV, the island's newest knockout artist.

2016 – 2019

The Undefeated Rise

From Hatillo to Kissimmee to San Juan, Bravo ran his record perfect — televised on Telemundo, DirecTV and Wapa TV. In March 2019 he captured the vacant WBC Latino Lightweight title at Coliseo Rafael G. Amalbert in Juncos.

2020 – 2022

The Big Stage

Premier Boxing Champions on FS1. Pay-per-view from Atlantic City. Then October 26, 2022 at Coliseo Roberto Clemente — a TKO of Adrian Yung to become WBO NABO Super Lightweight Champion in front of his people.

2025 – 2026

The Setback — and the Statement

A razor-thin split decision in March 2025 handed Bravo his first loss. Ten months later he walked into Barclays Center and erased every doubt — a second-round left-hook KO of Pedro Campa that made highlight reels worldwide. 24-1. Next stop: Campeón Mundial.

Beyond the Ring

Scholar & Fighter

Bravo holds a Bachelor's degree in Criminal Justice and is a prospective law student — proof that the discipline runs deeper than boxing. Trained by Angus Marksman. Bilingual in Spanish and English. Arecibo to the world.

Championship Titles

🏆 WBO NABO Super Lightweight

Captured the vacant WBO NABO title with a TKO of Adrian Yung (28-7-3) in October 2022 — announcing his arrival at 140 lbs.

🏆 WBC Latino Lightweight

Won the vacant WBC Latino belt with a unanimous decision over Emiliano Martin Garcia (16-3-0) in March 2019.

🏆 WBA Continental

Claimed the WBA Continental crown as an unbeaten sensation — the third belt in the Team Bravo trophy case.

🥊 Next: World Title

After the statement KO of Pedro Campa at Barclays Center, Bravo is positioned for a world title run at super lightweight. Campeón Mundial is the mission.
